Invest in the Future of Ethical Business

Apocrypha is not a charity or a fund. It's a systematic, ethical business creation engine—where being fair, collaborative, and long-term focused is a competitive advantage, not a restriction. We believe that helping others, especially fellow startups, and investing in each other when one has money and the other doesn't, is the best way to build a resilient, profitable ecosystem. Being good doesn't mean being poor or exploited; it means being fair, not greedy, and thinking in decades, not quarters.

Micro-Investors: The Power of the Pool

Imagine 1,000 people each investing $100–$500/month. Here's what that can do:

  • Monthly pool: $100,000 – $500,000
  • Annual pool: $1.2M – $6M
  • Lean MVP cost (volunteer teams, LLM/hosting only): $10,000 – $30,000
  • Seed-ready cost (MVP + traction): $50,000 – $150,000

How many startups can we launch?

  • At $1.2M/year: 40–120 MVPs, or 8–24 full-cycle startups (MVP → seed) per year
  • At $6M/year: 200–600 MVPs, or 40–120 full-cycle startups per year

With our community, every startup gets:

  • Instant access to hundreds of beta testers and market survey participants
  • Built-in audience for product launches
  • Real-time customer feedback and guidance
  • Much higher odds of seed selection and VC interest—VCs love proven customer traction

Systematic investment pools mean you can invest without feeling a burden, and you'll always see the ratio of micro-funders to startups in each pool. Your small monthly contribution can help launch dozens of real, lean, AI-powered businesses every year—businesses that reward contributors fairly and transparently.

Institutional Investors: Guidance, Not Control

We welcome industry guidance and partnership from institutional investors. But we are not looking for anyone to take over the core morality or governance of Apocrypha until our self-sustaining ethical mechanism is fully established. Your capital and expertise can help us scale, but the heart of the system—fairness, mutual support, and principled business—remains with the community.
